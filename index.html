TRANSIT PLUS+

ABOUT PROJECT

The Transit App helps parents and students find accurate bus routes and stop information. It also introduces a community-based carpooling feature, giving families more flexible commuting options.

TRANSIT APP THOUGHT PROCESS

Understanding the user was a crucial part throughout the design process. As someone who uses public transport, I already had some personal insight on the matter. Having a transit app that allows for carpooling and school buses was an interesting challenge, but first I had to find out if this was actually a problem people cared about. After carrying out extensive research with the aid of surveys and online public forums, I was certain that this was a challenge that required a modern solution.

CARPOOL SECTION

First being the carpool section. I took inspiration from various car-riding apps like Uber and Lyft. As this was a time I was beginning to refine my design skills, an important lesson I learned was you don’t need to reinvent the wheel. Building on this I was able to begin creating the layout for the app. Building off the original transit app, I was able to integrate both the carpool and the school bus features.

THE GOAL

Design a responsive, user-friendly app that:

  • Provides accurate and real-time bus route and stop information.
  • Enables safe and reliable community carpooling options.
  • Reduces stress for parents and students by simplifying commuting logistics.

USER RESEARCH

I conducted surveys and interviews with parents, students, and school transportation coordinators to understand their challenges. Initial assumption: Users mainly wanted to find bus stops and track arrival times. Key insight: Many parents were equally (or more) interested in safe carpooling options due to unreliable bus schedules and distant stops. This shifted the design focus toward integrating a community carpooling feature alongside the bus route system.

Key Pain Points

  • No Real-Time Updates – Parents waiting in poor weather without knowing bus arrival times.
  • Inconvenient Stops – Bus stops placed too far from homes or in unsafe areas.
  • Carpooling Barriers – Families struggled to find trusted, safe carpooling options.
  • Outdated Routes – Confusion and missed pickups due to unmaintained schedule data.
